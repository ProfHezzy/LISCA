Who We Are

About LISCA

Let It Shine Creative Academy (LISCA) is a non-profit educational initiative dedicated to providing free quality education, mentorship, and life-changing opportunities to children from underserved communities. Through academic support, practical skill acquisition, leadership development, and mentorship, LISCA helps children discover their potential and become future leaders.

Our Mission

To provide free access to quality education, practical skills, mentorship, and opportunities that empower children to achieve their dreams.

Our Vision

To raise a generation of confident, skilled, educated, and empowered young leaders.

Our Core Values

Education

Nurturing minds through rigorous and quality academic instruction.

Empowerment

Equipping children with the self-belief and skills to chart their own course.

Opportunity

Opening doors to internships, mentoring networks, and career pathways.

Community

Fostering teamwork, shared growth, and a strong local support system.

Innovation

Encouraging critical thinking, problem-solving, and creative applications.

Training youngsters to take responsibility and inspire others positively.

Service

Giving back to the community and driving positive social changes.

Real Stories, Real Growth

Creating Lasting Impact

Our interventions address structural inequalities, nurturing child leaders who can reshape their immediate communities.

Phase 1

Student Success Stories

Over 80% of our academic support students showed a letter-grade improvement within one term. Several have secured regional academic scholarships, transitioning successfully into prestigious high schools.

Phase 2

Community Development

LISCA hosts public vocational fairs and technical repairs run directly by students. We've equipped dozens of households with small scale home garden planters and provided shoes to impoverished youngsters.

Phase 3

Skills Acquisition

Practical skill-acquisition tracks ensure graduates hold concrete capabilities. Our students design actual websites, conduct data inventories for local shops, and run micro-farming operations.

Phase 4

Leadership Development

Weekly mentorship assemblies teach conflict resolution, design thinking, and social responsibilities. Students run their own internal committees, preparing them to lead public organizations tomorrow.
